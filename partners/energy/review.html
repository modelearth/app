Activated Carbon Production Facility

Project Goal - A facility design that generates more clean water, energy and air than it consumes.

The images below convey the scale of the building and the option for solar rooftops. Local Brunswick zoning allows up to 5 stories and could potentially be adjusted upward for special projects.



"The facility would be state-of-the-art and designed more like a micro-brewery than conventional carbon plants. All processing gasification and activation would take place indoors in a 100,000 sq ft, environmentally controlled building." - Nat Mundy

Structural Objectives:
Hurricane wind-resistant building profile.
Tsunami-resistant concrete pilings below columns.
Use of affordable, locally sourced wood products.

Suggested modifications to images above
Incorporation of pathways, leasable pastures and fast-growth forestry space.
5-story container storage areas for complimentary production facilities and Controlled Environment Agriculture (CEA).
Multiple smaller buildings with frontages to make space more dynamic and inviting.
Use of transparent wood on exterior frontages surrounding main building envelope.
Use of solar panels to provide shade canopies over parking and outdoor meeting areas.

Activated Carbonizer - Zero Landfill Sustainable Energy


Market overview for activated carbon. Source: Freedom Energy Tech

Water Purification

Carbon used in beds and filters to remove contaminants from drinking and wastewater.
MARKET: > $1B global and > $350M domestic U.S. market, projected to grow by more than 50% in next 5 years

Emission Controls

Carbon injected into emissions or used in vessels to remove air pollutants and odors.
MARKET: > $1B global and > $350M domestic U.S. market, projected to grow by more than 100% in next 5 years

Agricultural Applications

Carbon used in animal feed to remove toxins and soil to increase water and nutrient retention and improve crop yields.
MARKET: Emerging application with potential for > $5B domestic U.S. market
